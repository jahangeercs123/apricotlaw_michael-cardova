Practice Areas

Car Accidents

Vehicle collisions are a leading cause of injuries and death in the Phoenix area. Fight back against a reckless or careless driver by filing a car crash claim against them.

Truck Accidents

Commercial trucks can cause catastrophic injuries and devastating life changes for the victims involved. A trucking company, trucker, loading company, or parts manufacturer could be liable for your truck accident.

Medical Malpractice

When a doctor caused you harm because of subpar medical treatment, they can be held responsible for harming you. Medical negligence claims can also prevent others in Phoenix from being harmed by a careless doctor.

Premises Liability

Property owners have the responsibility of maintaining a safe property for guests and visitors. If a hazard on someone’s property went unaddressed and led to your injury, you can file a claim.

Dog Bites

Dog owners must be held accountable when they fail to restrain their pets. When a dangerous dog has injured you, you have every right to file a claim to receive damages.

Product Injuries

Dangerous products can cause serious injuries, and you can hold a negligent manufacturer accountable for your suffering. An attorney can investigate the product that caused you harm and do what’s possible to hold the manufacturer legally responsible.

Motorcycle Accidents

Many people don’t watch out for motorcyclists, and you can be seriously injured as a result. Make sure a negligent driver or other responsible party doesn’t go unpunished for causing your motorcycle crash.

Wrongful Death

Your beloved family member would still be here today if not for the careless or wrongful act of another. Receive justice for your loved one by filing a wrongful death claim.

Why You Can File a Claim When Someone Else Has Harmed You

The reason you are able to file a personal injury claim when you’ve been hurt in an accident you didn’t cause is because of Arizona’s negligence laws. The idea is that if someone else’s actions were responsible for causing you to be injured, they should be required to compensate you for what you went through. This law goes for unintentional injuries, as well.

For instance, if you are walking down the grocery store aisle and you slipped on a spill a worker left on the floor, the store worker who left the spill on the floor and the company should be held responsible. They likely didn’t leave the spill there with the intention of causing you harm, but they were still responsible for your injuries.

Who Pays Out on Personal Injury Claims?

When someone has injured you, you deserve to receive justice and compensation for what you have been through. Unfortunately, negligent people don’t always have the money to pay for your injuries and losses, even if they should be paying. Insurance was created as a way to protect injury victims financially when careless people cause you to be hurt

Property owners, vehicle drivers, businesses, and even the government usually have insurance policies so that when injuries and accidents do occur, the victims can receive monetary compensation. When you’ve been injured, it’s likely the at-fault party’s insurer that will be paying your settlement.

Many people have questions when they begin looking into filing an injury claim in Phoenix. When you contact our firm directly, you can receive case-specific answers during a free initial evaluation. However, to get a general idea of how the personal injury claim process works, here are a few common questions our law firm receives.

How long do I have to submit a personal injury claim in Arizona?

For most types of personal injury cases in Arizona, you are given two years to file your claim. The clock starts the day of your accident. You don’t want to wait, though, because two years flies by a lot faster than many people think. In addition, the faster you file your claim, the better chance you have that witnesses won’t disappear or evidence won’t get lost.

Will I have to go to court to win a settlement?

Most likely you will not need to set foot into a courtroom in order to win the settlement you deserve. Most Phoenix injury cases are settled outside of the courtroom. However, if it ever came to going to court to win the compensation you’re owed, your attorney at our firm will be ready to battle it out for you.

What are economic and non-economic damages?

All the monetary losses you’ve suffered as a result of your accident are often referred to as economic damages. These damages include things like medical bills, lost wages, costs of caregiving services, and property loss. Non-economic damages refer to any negative effect of your accident not tied to financial loss. For instance, pain and suffering are non-economic damages.

Is it really necessary for me to work with a lawyer?

Some claims can be filed without legal assistance, but it’s not usually a good idea. Your lawyer offers you invaluable legal know-how and experience. Working with a lawyer allows you to receive a settlement while your focus remains on what matters most—your health.

This is when your lawyer only gets paid for their services after they’ve won you the full settlement you deserve.
